Our Story

Technology & Company
Evolution Timeline

From a water treatment startup in Alberta to a fully integrated lithium technology platform — over a decade of continuous innovation in Direct Lithium Extraction.

2014 Origins of the Technology Platform
April 2014

PurLucid Treatment Solutions Founded

Dr. Preston McEachern forms PurLucid Treatment Solutions in Alberta, Canada. The company develops advanced water treatment technologies for oil sands produced water, deploying early systems with Canadian Natural Resources and entering engineering design phases with Pengrowth, Connacher, OSUM, and other producers.

2016 Lithium Recovery Breakthrough
February 2016

First Lithium Recovery Molecular Sieve Developed

Dr. McEachern develops the first lithium recovery molecular sieve at the University of Alberta Innovation Center for Engineering. The manganese-based material demonstrates high lithium selectivity, marking the genesis of what would become Empower's Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) platform.

June 2016

Alberta Innovates Funding & First Facility Opens

Funding received from Alberta Innovates to advance the filtration platform. The company opens its first facility for treatment of oil sands produced water and begins adapting filtration systems for lithium recovery, initiating early extraction testing on boiler blowdown concentrates.

2017–2018 Technology Development & First Lithium IP
October 2017

$14 Million Funding Raise

PurLucid raises $14 million in funding, including $8.2 million in federal and provincial support through NSERC — enabling significant expansion of research and pilot facilities.

June 2018

First Lithium Recovery Patent Filed

Dr. McEachern files his first lithium recovery patent, using an expanded bed design optimized for high-salinity produced water.

2020 Formation of the U.S. Lithium Platform
March 2020

AquaMin LLC Formed in the United States

Dr. McEachern forms AquaMin LLC to advance lithium extraction technologies in the U.S. and begins consulting with Berkshire Hathaway Energy Renewables (BHE Renewables) on lithium recovery from geothermal brines.

April 2020

LiMin Gen 1 Titanium Sieve Developed

AquaMin develops LiMin Gen 1, a titanium-based molecular sieve created at the University of Calgary, replacing earlier manganese materials due to concerns around fouling, durability, and toxicity.

May – October 2020

Salton Sea Field Pilot with BHE Renewables

AquaMin conducts extensive bench-scale testing on brines from around the world and develops a field pilot with BHE Renewables at the Salton Sea, demonstrating lithium recovery from geothermal brines at temperatures near 110°C with promising cycle life and selectivity.

2021 Creation of Conductive Energy
November 2021

AquaMin Merges with LiEP to Form Conductive Energy

AquaMin merges with LiEP in Calgary, combining lithium extraction materials science with process engineering and pilot operations. The newly formed Conductive Energy secures contracts with BHE Renewables to advance a pre-commercial lithium extraction demonstration facility at the Salton Sea.

2022 Global DLE Testing & First Commercial Pilots
Q1 – Q2 2022

TotalEnergies Global DLE Testing Agreement

Conductive Energy signs agreements with TotalEnergies to perform DLE testing on brines from multiple global assets. Bench-scale trials are completed successfully at Total's research center in France.

June 2022

BHE Renewables 400 tpa Facility Commissioned

The BHE Renewables pre-commercial 400 tpa lithium facility is commissioned at the Salton Sea utilizing Conductive Energy's Gen 3 DLE technology.

October 2022

TotalEnergies Texas DLE Pilot Deployed

Conductive deploys a 40 gpm DLE pilot system using its Gen 4 technology at a TotalEnergies site in Texas to process produced water from the Barnett shale.

2023 Expansion Toward Integrated Lithium Production
February 2023

Texas Field Pilot Successfully Completed

Conductive Energy completes its Texas field pilot with TotalEnergies, validating Gen 4 DLE technology performance across oilfield produced water conditions.

April 2023

U.S. Lithium Resource Evaluation Initiated

The company begins evaluating lithium resource opportunities in the United States while continuing to advance its lithium carbonate conversion capabilities.

October 2023

Merger Discussions with DME Energy Resources

Negotiations begin between Conductive Energy and DME Energy Resources regarding a potential partnership and U.S. lithium asset development.

2024 Lithium Conversion Technology
February 2024

Battery-Grade Lithium Carbonate Produced at Bench Scale

Conductive successfully produces battery-grade lithium carbonate at bench scale using brines sourced from Chile, demonstrating end-to-end extraction and conversion capability.

April 2024

CleanTech Lithium Contract Signed

A contract is signed with CleanTech Lithium to process Chilean DLE eluates into battery-grade lithium carbonate.

October – November 2024

Chicago Pilot Facility Commissioned

Conductive commissions a field pilot lithium carbonate facility in Chicago and successfully produces battery-grade lithium carbonate from DLE feedstock, proving the conversion process at pilot scale.

2025 Consolidation & U.S. Headquarters
February 2025

Conductive Energy & DME Agree to Merge

Conductive Energy and DME Energy Resources agree to merge operations and consolidate engineering, manufacturing, and lithium processing activities into a new 17,000 sq ft headquarters facility in Dallas, Texas.

October 2025

Merger Completed — Empower EIT Established

The merger between Conductive Energy and DME Energy Resources is completed. The combined company establishes a fully integrated lithium technology and processing platform in Dallas, with Chicago and Calgary operations relocated to Texas.

2025–2026 Formation of Empower EIT
December 2025

Series A Funding of $10 Million Secured

Empower receives Series A funding of $10 million, with a $6 million commitment from Joe Williams, President & Co-Founder of Keller Williams International. Mr. Williams accepts a seat on Empower's Board of Directors.

February 2026

Organization Rebrands as Empower EIT

The Dallas facility nears commissioning and the organization rebrands as Empower EIT, reflecting the integration of lithium extraction, conversion, and engineering technologies developed over more than a decade.

March 2026

Full Lithium Value Chain Demonstrated

Empower begins processing CleanTech Lithium eluates from Chile into battery-grade lithium carbonate, demonstrating the company's complete lithium value chain capability from extraction through final conversion.
